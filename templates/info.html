Understanding Alzheimer's

A comprehensive guide to symptoms, stages, and modern treatment options.

Common Symptoms

Memory Loss

Forgetting recently learned information is the most common early sign.

Problem Solving Issues

Difficulties in developing and following a plan or working with numbers.

Time & Place Confusion

Losing track of dates, seasons, and the passage of time.

Disease Stages

Mild / Early Stage
STAGE 1

A person may function independently but has memory lapses, such as forgetting familiar words or the location of objects.

Moderate / Middle Stage
STAGE 2

Usually the longest stage. The person may confuse words, get frustrated or angry, or act in unexpected ways, such as refusing to bathe.

Severe / Late Stage
STAGE 3

In the final stage, individuals lose the ability to respond to their environment, to carry on a conversation and, eventually, to control movement.

Prevention Tips

  • Heart-healthy diet
  • Regular exercise
  • Cognitive stimulation
  • Social engagement
  • Quality sleep
Medical Advice

If you or a loved one is experiencing these symptoms, consult a neurologist immediately. Early detection significantly improves management options.
